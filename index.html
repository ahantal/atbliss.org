Our Mission

Safe, playful, and inclusive spaces for all

To create safe, playful, and inclusive spaces where people of all ages remember their pure inner essence, heal in community, and explore the shared wisdom of diverse traditions — awakening love, compassion, and oneness in everyday life.

"Awakening love, compassion, and oneness
in everyday life."

Our Vision

A world where every human being feels deeply connected to themselves, to each other, and to the One — living with open hearts, courageous authenticity, and a shared sense of sacred unity beyond all labels, beliefs, and divisions.

Our Work

The Four Pillars

Pure Inner Essence

The inner work. Awareness, compassion, courage, and play — helping every person of every age reconnect with the divine essence they carry within. The foundation of everything @Bliss offers.

Pillar 03

Healing Arts

A living sanctuary of healing modalities, held always in community. Each practice is a door — not a destination — into deeper self-remembering. Sessions are open to all.

I
Somatic Breathwork

Conscious breathing to release stored tension, trauma, and emotion from the body. Held in a safe, guided group container — the first physical expression of the @Bliss healing sanctuary.

II
Yoga

An ancient practice of union — body, breath, and awareness in alignment. Offered in community as a moving meditation and a gateway into the present moment.

III
Sound Baths

Immersive sound healing using singing bowls, gongs, and resonant instruments. Sound as a vehicle for deep relaxation, nervous system reset, and expanded awareness.

IV
Tai Chi

Slow, intentional movement as meditation. Cultivating internal energy, balance, and a profound sense of ease and presence — accessible to all ages and bodies.

V
Dance

Movement as liberation. Conscious dance creates space for authentic self-expression, joy, and release — returning the body to its natural intelligence and aliveness.

VI
Somatic Work

Body-centred practices that support the integration of emotion and experience. Somatic work acknowledges that healing lives in the body, not only in the mind.

VII
Plant Medicine Ceremonies

Sacred ceremonies held with reverence and care. Plant medicine as an ancient teacher — offered in intentional community, with full support before, during, and after.

"Each modality is offered as a door — not a destination — into deeper self-remembering."

Pillar 04

Community Life

Bliss is also found in the shared rhythm of ordinary life. These programmes make @Bliss accessible to everyone — a gateway to belonging for those who may not yet come for healing or ceremony.

1
Swim School

Making water safety and swimming accessible to children and families. An entry point into the @Bliss community for those who come not for ceremony, but for their children — and find something more.

2
Cooking Classes

The kitchen as a place of connection and nourishment — not just of the body but of community. Cooking together as an ancient practice of belonging and care.

3
Farming & Land Work

Returning to the earth. Working with land, seasons, and growth as a practice of presence, humility, and deep connection to the living world that sustains us.

4
Everyday Skills

Practical skills that build belonging and self-reliance. Programmes that meet people where they are — accessible, grounding, and offered in the spirit of shared life.

Manifesto

What we believe

We believe every human being carries a divine, pure essence within — and that remembering this essence becomes possible through awareness, compassion, courage, and a sense of play.

We approach different religions, philosophies, and ancient wisdoms not as competing systems, but as different paths opening to the same Truth — and we seek to reveal the bridges between them.

@Bliss does not seek to replace any religion or tradition. It exists to make visible the consciousness of Oneness and universal compassion that lives at the heart of them all.

@Bliss is a space of re-membering together, where children, parents, and all seekers can show up as they are, without judgment — and allow heart, mind, and body to heal and expand together.

In every practice, every conversation, every gathering, we ask:

"What do I not know — or not remember — to bring me, and us, a little closer to the One, to love, and to truth. To Bliss?"
